Books I am reading

  • Adventures in Group Theory: Rubik’s Cube, Merlin’s Machine, and Other Mathematical Toys by David Joyner

    • This is a pleasurable read. I am slowly and admiringly wandering through this text.

  • Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms by David McKay

    • David McKay strikes a perfect balance between theory and application. This book is excellent and I expect to continually return to it. I watched most of his video lectures on YouTube too. If you're looking to get into the "Machine Learning" discussion, a solid grounding in probability, information theory and coding theory is essential.

  • The Art of Computer Programming by Donald Knuth Volumes 1, 4a and 4b

    • I'm mainly into puzzles and recreational maths at the moment so I'm focusing on these volumes. I am trying to work through all the exercises rated from 00 to 30. I am also reading the fasicles on Constraint Satisfaction Problems and the collection of puzzles. He writes well, I like his style.

    I think Knuth knows that he won't be able to finish writing about sequential programming in his lifetime and so has decided to become a recreational mathematician, which is excellent news for me.

    Check out some of the problems I'm doing with Wyan here!

  • Information, Physics and Computation by Andrea Montanari and Marc Mézard.

    • I used this book as a guide to look up the relevant topics in other books and papers. The exposition is sketchy at times, but clear and suitable for beginners like me.

  • An Introduction to Mathematical Logic by Herbert Enderton

    • A poorly written book, but your options are limited if you want to learn logic. Honestly, I recommend sitting in on a few classes or taking a course like I did.

  • Linear Algebra Done Right by Sheldon Axler
